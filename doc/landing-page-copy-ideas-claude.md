## Feature Sections

### "Hire any bot with ease" / Bring Your Own Agent

**Headline ideas:**
- Bring your own agent.
- Any agent. Any runtime. Anywhere.
- Hire any bot with ease.
- Your agents, your way. We just run the company.
- OpenClaw. Claude. Codex. Cursor. A bash script. We don't care.

**Copy ideas:**
- Paperclip is the control plane, not the execution plane. Your agents run wherever they run — Python scripts, Claude Code sessions, OpenClaw bots, HTTP endpoints, Node processes. Paperclip just needs one thing: a way to call them.
- Two adapters out of the box: process (spawn a local command) and HTTP (hit a webhook). That's the entire contract. If your agent is callable, it works with Paperclip.
- We don't lock you into a runtime. We don't force a framework. Build your agent however you want, plug it in, and Paperclip handles the rest — identity, org placement, budget, tasks, and governance.
- Connect agents from anywhere: OpenClaw, Cursor, Codex, Claude Code, custom scripts. If it can receive a heartbeat, it's hired.

### Goal Alignment / "Keep your agents aligned"

**Headline ideas:**
- Every task traces to the goal.
- No busywork. No drift. Just execution toward the target.
- From company mission to individual task — in one chain.
- Keep your agents aligned to the goal.
- Goals cascade. Agents execute. You stay in control.

**Copy ideas:**
- Paperclip enforces a strict hierarchy: company goal → team goals → agent goals → tasks. Every piece of work your agents do traces back to the company mission. If it doesn't connect, it doesn't belong.
- Your CEO agent breaks down the company goal into strategy. Managers break strategy into projects. Engineers break projects into tasks. At every level, everyone knows *why* they're working.
- This isn't task management. It's goal management. The org chart isn't decoration — it's the delegation chain that ensures alignment from boardroom to codebase.

### Agent Governance / Approvals

**Headline ideas:**
- Human in the loop. Always.
- You're the board. Act like it.
- Approve hires. Approve strategy. Override anything.
- Governance built in, not bolted on.
- Autonomous doesn't mean unsupervised.

**Copy ideas:**
- You operate as the board of directors. Agents can't hire new agents without your approval. The CEO can't execute a strategy you haven't reviewed. You can pause any agent, reassign any task, adjust any budget — at any time.
- Two approval gates by default: agent hiring and CEO strategic breakdown. Your agents propose. You decide. No work starts until you say go.
- Pause. Resume. Override. Reassign. Terminate. You have full control over every agent in the org. Autonomy is a privilege you grant, not a default.

### Cost Tracking & Budgets

**Headline ideas:**
- Know what every agent costs. Control what every agent spends.
- Token budgets with teeth.
- Budgets that enforce themselves.
- Cost control at the agent level.
- Your agents have salaries. Set them.

**Copy ideas:**
- Every agent gets a monthly budget. When they hit it, they stop. Automatically. No runaway costs. No surprise bills. Hard limits, enforced by the system.
- Track costs per agent, per task, per project, per goal. See which agents are expensive, which tasks burn tokens, which projects are over budget. Real cost attribution — not estimates.
- Budget delegation follows the org chart. You set the company budget. The CEO allocates to departments. Managers allocate to their reports. Everyone operates within their means.
- Provider-level tracking: see spend by model (Claude, GPT, etc.), by token type (input vs. output), by billing code. Finance-grade visibility into your AI workforce costs.

## "What Paperclip Is Not" Section

**Headline ideas:**
- What Paperclip is not.
- Let's be clear about what this isn't.
- Not another AI tool.

**Ideas for this section (list of negatives):**
- **Not a code review tool.** Paperclip doesn't review your pull requests. It runs your company.
- **Not a chatbot.** There's no conversation interface. Agents have jobs, not chat windows.
- **Not an agent framework.** We don't tell you how to build agents. We tell you how to run a company made of them.
- **Not a workflow builder.** No drag-and-drop pipelines. Paperclip models companies — with org charts, goals, budgets, and governance.
- **Not a prompt manager.** Agents bring their own prompts, models, and runtimes. Paperclip manages the organization they work in.
- **Not a single-agent tool.** This is for teams. Hierarchies. Companies. If you have one agent, you probably don't need Paperclip. If you have twenty — you definitely do.
- **Not SaaS.** Self-hosted. Open source. Your data stays on your infrastructure.

## The "How It Works" Walkthrough

**Option A — Three steps:**
1. **Define the goal.** "Build the #1 AI note-taking app to $1M MRR."
2. **Hire the team.** CEO, CTO, engineers, designers, marketers — all agents, all configured to your spec.
3. **Approve and run.** Review the CEO's strategy. Set budgets. Hit go. Monitor from the dashboard.

**Option B — Five steps:**
1. Create a company.
2. Set the company goal.
3. Hire a CEO agent (OpenClaw, Claude, custom — your choice).

5. Agents execute. You govern.

**Option C — The operator narrative:**

The CEO proposes: hire a CTO, CMO, and three engineers. You approve. The CEO breaks the goal into team objectives. The CTO breaks engineering objectives into sprints. Engineers pick up tasks, execute, report back. You see it all in real time — costs, progress, blockers, decisions. When something needs your input, an approval lands in your queue. You're the board. They're the company.

## Competitive Positioning Ideas

**vs. CrewAI / LangGraph / AutoGen:**
- Those are agent frameworks. Paperclip is company infrastructure. They help you build one agent pipeline. Paperclip helps you run an entire organization of agents with governance, budgets, and accountability.

**vs. Linear / Jira / Asana:**
- Task management software assumes human workers. Paperclip assumes AI workers. Different problem. Different solution. Agents need heartbeats, budgets, and approval gates — not Kanban boards.

**vs. Building it yourself:**
- You could wire up agents, task queues, cost tracking, approval workflows, and audit logging yourself. Or you could run `npx paperclip onboard` and have it in five minutes.
